iWonder Audio

BBC Learning

While working on the BBC Learning Innovations Team, I lead a project exploring binaural audio. The objective was to build a prototype demonstrator to assess value to iWonder’s audiences. Below is an overview of how we came to the final prototype and proposal for for development of iWonder Audio.

Visual Design

iWonder Audio had to fit seamlessly into the broader iWonder product, with style and user experience being on par. Referenceing BBC’s universal GEL style and the iWonder Style Guide, I worked with stakeholders to establish style and decide on key features.

Custom Beats

One of the big questions that arose while developing the product was weather to incorporate a feature where audiences could construct their own binaural beats. Stakeholders were keen to show the science behind the audio through this feature, but there were concerns audiences would be confused or put off by the feature. After developing a ‘mood only’ version, the decision was made to keep the custom beats feature.

Demonstrator Development

Using Tumult Hype, we developed a lean demonstrator version of iWonder Audio for presentation. A few features intended for the final version were not included due to time/budget such as the volume slider, which was redesigned for tap, and the custom beats options were streamlined to suit what could be coded before deadline.

Proposal

A detailed presentation revealing our findings and proposal for the development of the final product was delivered to very positive review and excitement for potential development, particularly the opportunities to partner with different parts of the BBC for curation of audio. I have since moved on from BBC Learning but look on with great interest to see if the product will get the go ahead.
