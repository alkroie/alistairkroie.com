Manky Manor

Nickelodeon

I was responsible for developing the interactive components for a pilot series called Manky Manor. I took the approach of packaging the episode into 3-D style user interface where a mini-game and extra and behind the scenes content could also be accessed.

TV vs. Interactive

I worked closely with the Creative Director to establish a production strategy where we could film interactive content during downtime on the production. We produced a mini-game, where audiences could interact with one of the scenarios in the episode. My work fit seemlessly into the production. We utilised existing resources to add a new dimension of interactivity, enabling audiences to partisipate.

Behind the Scenes

Extra video content including interviews, and behind the scenes content were also incorperated. The project was a successful example of developing interactive content around a TV production. It was also a blue-print for further behind the scenes content production for series such as Camp Orange.
