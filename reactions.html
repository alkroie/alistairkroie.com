Reactions

BBC News

BBC News Reactions is a project taking social media embedding on the BBC News website & app to another level. Rather than users logging into their social media accounts outside BBC News to see what people are saying, we explored embedding the most reacted to posts from different social media platforms within a lovable UI.

Persona & User Story

Existing embedding of individual tweets on BBC News often doesn't reflect a variety of audience attitudes. Our challenge was to make available a range of popular social media posts via an easy to use UI within the BBC News website & app. Personas were developed to identify who we were making the prototype for and their needs. User stories were also developed to clarify the product goals.

User Journey

A variety of possible user journeys were developed. We developed two solutions, having a reactions area where users could explore posts via top news stories and an area within a news story where users could acess related reactions.

Wireframes and Ideation

Using Sketch and Illustrator I developed wireframes depicting different layout options. I developed a feature list based on the user journeys with a developer to ensure what I was proposing was achievable.

Early User Testing

Three seperate designs were developed that we put to audiences for testing in face to face interviews. We looked at how the products would keep audiences on the BBC website. Asthetics, ease of use, and if users would return to use the proposed products were reviewed in testing.

Interactive Prototype

For the purposes of further user testing and clearly conveying our desired final outcome to stakeholders and developers, I created a functioning prototype of the layout responded to most positively in user testing. I left the BBC after delivering this prototype and a detailed proposal but am hopeful it will be developed.
